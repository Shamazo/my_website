More About Me

I am an Australian-American-Canadian citzien currently residing in Cambridge, Massachusetts. I am a Junior at Harvard College studying Computer Science with a secondary in Statistics.

I grew up in Mount Eliza, which is a small town just out side of Melbourne, Victoria. I am interested in both computer science theory, including information thoery, coding theory and theoretical machine learning as well as applying computer science to other fields, especially the natural sciences which frequently have astronomical data sets (pun intended). Outside of academics I am a photographer, DJ, concert goer and audio equipment enthusiast. Depending on my workload, you can catch me hosting a radio show at WHRB 95.3FM, Harvard's fully student run radio station where I am also the tech director. I listen to everything from Australian psychedelic rock and French Rap to European Techno and peacful soundscapes.

My Work Experience

June 2018 - August 2018

NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory

Summer Undergraduate Research Fellow

At JPL I worked in the Planetary and Exoplanetary Atmosphers group (3222) with Dr. Glenn Orton. I conducted research on fine scale waves in Jupiter's atmosphere by analyzing data from the JunoCam instrument, which is the visible spectrum camera on the Juno orbiter. I determined the statistical range of the waves as well as properties of specific waves. I also generated the largest catalogue of finescale waves in the visible spectrum on Jupiter to date.

Febuary 2018 - Present

Bereau of Study Counsel

Peer Tutor

Depending on the semester, I am either tutoring students in CS50 - Introduction to Computer Science or CS51 - Abstraction and Design in Computation. CS50 covers rudiemntary computer science concepts in C, Python, SQL and Javascript while CS51 focuses on methods of abstraction such as object oriented programming, functional programming and imperative programming in OCaml.
